Synopsis

The current format is about a high school concept, where the cast members act like students in a classroom, while the guests would come as freshly transferred students. To be able to accept the new students, the cast reads their history and thanks to that they give or not the approval to transfer. Once accepted into the new school, the guests ask the cast a series of questions about themselves so that they can answer them and thus learn more about them. Subsequently, the program is sometimes diversified in the end with a segment outside the school concept.

One of the most captivating attributes of this format is the informal and banmal (casual language) language used by everyone, regardless of age or seniority, which disregards Korea's strict age rules with spoken language. Informal chat also encourages guests and cast to interact with each other as students often do, leading them to feel comfortable enough to annoy each other.

SEGMENTS

Permanent Sections

Membership Request:

A segment in which the guests previously filled out a transfer form in which one of the cast members read it and the others commented on it. The highlight of this segment is the guests' preference over which cast members they would rather sit next to.

Guess about me:

segment where the guests prepared various questions related to themselves. Some of the questions are vague or extremely private which the guests never revealed to the public.

Other Sections

Second Period:

Generally comes after the permanent segments and varies by guests. In most cases, guests made suggestions for activities in this segment. It varies between counseling sessions, art classes, and physical education classes.

Let's Play:

Generally comes after the permanent segments and varies by guests. In most cases, guests made suggestions for activities in this segment. It varies between counseling sessions, art classes, and physical education classes.

Generally comes after the permanent segments and varies by guests. In most cases, guests made suggestions for activities in this segment. It varies between counseling sessions, art classes, and physical education classes.

Music Class:

Generally comes after the permanent segments and varies by guests. In most cases, guests made suggestions for activities in this segment. It varies between counseling sessions, art classes, and physical education classes.

The Lee Sang Min Moment:

formally featured in ep. 56 and previously called "Choose Your Ideal Type", is a short segment in which guests (usually women) receive information from Lee Sang Min to choose any of the cast members (plus any accompanying male guests) as their Ideal pair.

Dances-tagram:

formally presented in ep. 57 by Chanmi (AOA), but in an episode with Psy it became a segment where the PD plays the initial syllable / note of a song and they have to guess the title, the lyrics and the movements of the dance..

99-second teamwork competition:

a segment that was often used when a large group of people participated in the show, such as idol groups. The segment varies each episode in terms of missions, but the team of members and guests must complete them in 99 seconds.
